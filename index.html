How it works & troubleshooting

PeerJS backend (default). Each receiver gets a short peer ID brokered by PeerJS's free signaling cloud. The phone connects to that ID and data flows peer-to-peer via WebRTC. Works across networks (cellular ↔ Wi-Fi) because PeerJS bundles TURN relay in their free tier. The signaling cloud touches only the connection setup, not your data.

Raw WebRTC backend. Pure peer-to-peer with no third-party servers (just Google STUN). Devices exchange a full WebRTC handshake via QR codes — desktop shows offer chunks, phone scans them, phone shows answer chunks, desktop's webcam scans those. Only works on same Wi-Fi. Useful for offline-ish situations or if you want zero infrastructure dependency.

Same origin required. Both devices must open this page from the same URL. IndexedDB is partitioned per origin.

If PeerJS fails: their cloud may be down. Switch to Raw WebRTC and put both devices on the same Wi-Fi.
